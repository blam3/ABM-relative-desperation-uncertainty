# Model Rename — Notes

The current acronym **DMAP** (Decision-Making under Ambiguity and Poverty) no longer reflects the model's framing, which has shifted from ambiguity specifically toward uncertainty more broadly. The following options are proposed for discussion.

## Options

### Descriptive (what the model does)

| Name | Expansion | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **DRUM** | Decision-making under Risk and Uncertainty Model | Captures the risk + uncertainty framing; clean acronym |
| **RAMP** | Risk and Ambiguity under Material Poverty | Retains the ambiguity thread while broadening to material poverty |
| **DEPTH** | Desperation, Economics, Poverty, and Threshold Heuristics | Names the threshold mechanism explicitly |

### Conceptual (the core mechanism)

| Name | Expansion | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| **RelDep** | Relative Deprivation model | Short, maps directly to the relative deprivation theory the model operationalizes |
| **DesThresh** | Desperation Threshold | Names the de Courson et al. (2025) framework the model is built on |
| **PRUM** | Poverty, Relative deprivation, Uncertainty Model | Comprehensive but longer |

## Recommendation

**RelDep** or **DesThresh** — both are short, theoretically grounded, and directly traceable to the empirical literature. **DesThresh** has the advantage of naming the specific framework (desperation threshold model) rather than the broader theory.

## References

- de Courson, B., Frankenhuis, W. E., & Nettle, D. (2025). Poverty is associated with both risk avoidance and risk taking: empirical evidence for the desperation threshold model from the UK and France. *Proceedings of the Royal Society B*, 292(2040), 20242071.
